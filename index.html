Indianapolis Public Schools is honored to celebrate our top scholars in the district each year: our valedictorians and salutatorians!

The 2018 Vals & Sals have overcome many obstacles — from childhood trauma, to depression and even immigrating to a new land — to make it to the top of their class academically.

Please help us congratulate these amazing youths.

Arlington High School

Salutatorian: Joi Moseley

Joi Moseley, a member of the National Honor Society, is a native of Barbados in the Caribbean Islands. In the fall, Joi will study Culinary Arts at Ivy Tech Community College. She plans to become a restauranteur.

Valedictorian: Tinisha (Barran) Simpson

Valedictorian Tinisha (Barran) Simpson has been involved in cheerleading, volleyball and cross country. She also is a member of student government, the National Honor Society and has served as social awareness officer of the JAG (Jobs for America’s Graduates) program. Tinisha, who wants to become an anesthesiologist, will study biology/pre-med at IUPUI in the fall.

Salutatorian: Mariah Voyles

Salutatorian Mariah Voyles, a former John Marshall student, serves as secretary of JAG (Jobs for America’s Graduates). She is a member of student government and the school’s choir. In the fall, Mariah will attend either Ivy Tech Community College or IUPUI to major in Elementary Education and minor in Culinary Arts.

Valedictorian: Linaizisa McNeal

Valedictorian Linaizisa McNeal, a former John Marshall student, is a member of the National Honor Society. She has aspirations of opening a real estate agency. Linaizisa will major in business at either Ivy Tech Community College or Bennett College, an all-girls school in North Carolina.

Valedictorian Rachael L. Steelman is a member of the National Honor Society and has played both softball and volleyball at Arsenal Tech. In the fall, Rachael will attend the University of Indianapolis to major in computer science.
